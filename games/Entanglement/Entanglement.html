⚛ What Is Quantum Entanglement?

Quantum entanglement occurs when two particles share a single quantum state.

Measurements on one particle become correlated with measurements on the other particle.

The particles do not send messages to each other. Instead, they were prepared in a shared quantum state.

This principle is used in:

  • Quantum Communication
  • Quantum Cryptography
  • Quantum Networking
  • Quantum Teleportation Research

📖 Bell State

|Φ⁺⟩ = (|00⟩ + |11⟩)/√2

This means both particles exist in a superposition until measurement.

When measured, outcomes become correlated.
